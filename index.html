The Itinerary

Base Camp · 4225 Big Horn Lane, Island Park, ID 83429 · September 4–8, 2026

Goal: Stock the cabin and prep for the 5:15 AM Saturday launch.

  • 2:30 PMGroceries in Rexburg — Broulim’s or Albertsons in Rexburg, ID — your last full-scale grocery store.
    Must-haves: bear spray, cooler ice, sandwich supplies, high-protein snacks.
  • 4:00 PMCabin Check-In — Arrive at 4225 Big Horn Lane.
  • 6:00 PMDinner in Island Park — Ponds Lodge or Lakeside Lodge (both close to Bill’s Island).
  • 8:30 PMPrep — Pack the cooler, set coffee for 4:45 AM, and gas up in Island Park.

Route: West Entrance → Hayden Valley → Canyon → Mammoth → Lamar Valley

  • 5:15 AMLeave cabin — Crucial to beat the West Entrance line.
  • 6:30–8:30 AMHayden Valley — Prime time for bears and the Wapiti Lake wolf pack. Focus on Alum Creek pull-offs.
  • 10:00 AMGrand Canyon of the Yellowstone — Hit Artist Point first for the iconic view.
  • 1:00 PMMammoth Hot Springs — Walk the Upper Terraces. Watch for elk on the lawns.
  • 5 PM–DuskLamar Valley — The “best odds” window.
    Strategic spot: Slough Creek or Hitching Post — look for the spotting-scope crowds.
  • LateReturn to Island Park — ~2-hour drive. Watch for moose/elk on Hwy 20.

Route: West Entrance → Grand Prismatic → Old Faithful → West Thumb

  • 6:00 AMLeave cabin
  • 7:00 AMGrand Prismatic Overlook — Hike the Fairy Falls trail early — this lot is the first to fill on Labor Day weekend.
  • 10:30 AMOld Faithful & Upper Geyser Basin — Watch one eruption, walk to Morning Glory Pool, exit before the noon rush.
  • 2:00 PMWest Thumb Geyser Basin — Stunning lakeside geysers.
  • 3:30 PMLake Butte Overlook — Short, steep drive to a panoramic view of the 132-sq-mile lake.
  • 5:00 PMPelican Creek Nature Trail — Flat 0.6-mile loop through forest and marsh to a quiet beach. Great for birding.
  • 6:30 PMHayden Valley (Round 2) — Use the magic-hour light to scan for predators you missed Saturday.
  • 7:30 PMSunset Picnic — Dinner at a pull-out as the bison herds settle for the night.
  • 8:00 PMDepart park — A dark, quiet drive back to Island Park — high beams on!

Route: Island Park → Ashton → Teton Valley → Jackson

  • 5:30 AMLeave cabin
  • ShortcutHwy 20 S → Hwy 32 through Ashton — Bypasses the park gates and offers the best “backside” views of the Tetons.
  • 7:30 AMOxbow Bend — Best chance for moose and the famous “mirror” reflection.
  • 9:00 AMJenny Lake — Head straight for the boat shuttle — at capacity by 10 AM.
  • 1:00 PMMormon Row & Schwabacher Landing — Classic photography stops.
  • EveningMoose-Wilson Road — Slow, narrow drive — excellent for black bears in the berry bushes.
  • DinnerJackson, WY — A nicer meal to close out the trip.
  1. Bear spray: always on your person, not in your bag.
  2. Distance: stay 100 yards (91 m) from bears/wolves and 25 yards (23 m) from bison/elk.
  3. Commute: Bill’s Island is ~40 minutes from the West Entrance — factor it into every morning.
  4. Fuel: never let your tank drop below half inside the park.

Possible add-on: Upper Mesa Falls — 52 minutes south of the cabin.
